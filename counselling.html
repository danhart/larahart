Counselling

Many people seek counselling or longer-term therapy at different times of their life. For some, this may be at times of distress, unhappiness or confusion. Others seek counselling to explore themselves and their relationships to gain greater awareness and understanding of how they are being and relating in the world. Others are looking for a stronger sense of direction to live a more fulfilled, authentic life.

Counselling offers a safe place in which to explore, thoughts, feelings and experiences, in confidence, with a counsellor who will listen in a non-judgemental and supportive way. You will have the opportunity to turn your attention towards yourself. In developing greater awareness of how you meet your present needs, new choices emerge leading to personal growth and the possibility of change in your life. Counselling can help you express difficult emotions and explore specific issues, such as:

  • anger, difficulty with expressing feelings
  • anxiety, stress, panic
  • bereavement, grief and loss
  • depression, negative thoughts and feelings
  • identity and self-esteem issues
  • life transitions
  • loneliness and isolation
  • obsessions, compulsions and addictions
  • self-development
  • work difficulties including bullying
